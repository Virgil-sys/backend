History of Victoria Falls

Discover the fascinating past, local legends, and highlights of the mighty Victoria Falls region and its iconic locationsas well as the cultures that have lived here for centuries.

  • Main Falls Story: The world's largest sheet of falling water, named "Mosi-oa-Tunya" or "The Smoke That Thunders" by locals.
  • Livingstone Island: Where explorer David Livingstone first viewed the Falls in 1855.
  • Devil's Pool: Natural rock pool perched on the edge, swimmable in low water season.
  • Victoria Falls Bridge: 1905 engineering marvel, linking Zimbabwe and Zambia.
  • Local Culture: Tonga, Lozi, and Ndebele people, whose crafts and traditions thrive here.
  • Wildlife: The rainforest ecosystem and diverse animals supported by the constant spray of the falls.
